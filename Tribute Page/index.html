A brief introduction about the person being honored.

About David Goggins

David Goggins is a retired Navy SEAL, ultra-endurance athlete, and motivational speaker known for his extraordinary mental toughness and perseverance. Born on February 17, 1975, in Buffalo, New York, Goggins had a challenging childhood marked by poverty, abuse, and racism. Despite these obstacles, he went on to become one of the most accomplished endurance athletes in the world, setting records in events like the 24-hour pull-up challenge and completing numerous ultramarathons. Goggins joined the U.S. Air Force before ultimately transitioning to the Navy SEALs, where he endured grueling physical and mental challenges. His time in the military was marked by multiple deployments and a reputation for pushing the limits of what was considered humanly possible. He later became a popular motivational speaker and the author of the bestselling book Can't Hurt Me, in which he shares his life story and the mindset that helped him overcome seemingly insurmountable obstacles. David Goggins is known for his "never quit" mentality and his ability to face pain and adversity head-on. His message is one of resilience, discipline, and the power of the mind in overcoming personal and professional challenges.

Achievements

  • Completed Navy SEAL BUD/S training (3 times)
  • Ran numerous ultramarathons, including the Badwater 135
  • Set a Guinness World Record for pull-ups (4,030 in 24 hours)
  • Wrote the best-selling book Can't Hurt Me
  • Participated in over 50 combat missions in Iraq and Afghanistan
  • Completed Hell Week multiple times during SEAL training
